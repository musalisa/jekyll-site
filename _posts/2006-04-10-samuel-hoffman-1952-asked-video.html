You Asked For It was a human-interest show that aired between 1950 and 1959. For the first few months, it was named The Art Baker Show, after the series creator and host, Art Baker. In April of 1951, the show's title was changed to You Asked For It. [Wikipedia]

In 1952 the show featured Samuel Hoffman playing "a dance melody that he recently recorded for Capitol Records", as Art Baker said presenting him.

The quality of the video is very bad, nonetheless is a precious and rare document of Sam Hoffman and his unique style.
